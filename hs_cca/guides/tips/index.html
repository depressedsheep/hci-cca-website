CCA Guides

Choice of CCA is crucial. The CCA you have chosen should hopefully be your commitment for the next 4 years in Hwa Chong (High School)

Doing well in your CCA requires commitment, but you must of course find balance between schoolwork, social/family life and rest.

Here are some tips and important information to help you choose your CCA.

Choosing your CCA

It is very important that you choose your CCA wisely and try your best to get into the CCAs of your choice.

As such, here is a compiled list of tips to help your in your decision making.

  1. Be Informed about the CCAs

    Hwa Chong offers 40 CCAs, from 4 different categories (Sports, Uniform Groups, Aesthetics and Clubs & Societies).

    With so many CCAs to choose from, It is crucial that you browse through all the CCAs so you can make a well-informed decision.

    You should also look through and apply for CCAs even if you think you have no chance of getting in. Perhaps you may find your potential and flair in that CCA.

    You can read and browse through the CCAs here.

  2. Make a list of the CCAs you are interested in

    It is wise to make a list of all the CCAs that you are interested in (after browsing through). Try to include CCAs from different categories as much as possible.

    This compiled list will come in handy when you are asked to choose your CCA via the online portal.

    Apply for all these CCAs and all the best on getting that confirmed offer!

  3. Weigh your interest and abilities

    The nature of CCAs in Hwa Chong are competitive.

    There is therefore a need to have both ability and interest when choosing a CCA.

    However, do note that having an ability for a CCA does not necessarily mean having a particular skillset. It could just be the passion for learning.

  4. Parents' Expectations and Your Own Interests

Differences between Primary and Secondary School System

*Note that some of the CCA systems may differ for primary schools
Primary School Hwa Chong
CCA Requirement

Optional

If I want a CCA, I can join one, but if I do not want a CCA, I can just choose not to have one *

Compulsory

Every student is required to take up at least 1 CCA. (More than 1 CCA is not recommended)
Maximum no. of CCAs

Unlimited

As many CCAs as we want *

1 CCA

Every student is advised to take up only one CCA.

However, 2 CCAs are also allowed on a case-by-case basis.
Quitting

Can quit anytime

If I feel that this CCA is not suitable for me, I can just quit/change my CCA *

Must be sustained over 4 years in High School

If you quit your original CCA, then the development that particular CCA will not be complete, and you will end up not being able to acquire the relevant skills and maximise your potential fully.
Nature of CCA

Recreational/Enrichment

CCAs are mainly recreational or just for student's exposure.

Competitive & recreational

Structure and Development more complete

Through the four years in the CCA, you will be able to build our character and hone your skills and in a particular area.
Attendance

Lenient

In most cases, attendance not taken

Stringent

Must meet a certain percentage of attendance

Teachers/ExCo will keep track of attendance and there must be a valid excuse (MC, school events) for skipping CCA.
CCA Allocation

First come, First serve

If the students register for a CCA, they will get into the CCA unless all slots available have been filled up. *

Weighing between interest and ability

Since the nature of the CCA is competitive, most CCAs will conduct selection tests to seek out students with both ability and interest
