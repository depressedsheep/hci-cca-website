CCA Guides

Choice of CCA is crucial. The CCA you have chosen should hopefully be your commitment for the next 4 years in Hwa Chong (High School)

Doing well in your CCA requires commitment, but you must of course find balance between schoolwork, social/family life and rest.

This page will contain some guides to help you choose the right CCA.

CCA Life

After you have chosen your CCA, you are sure to spend a substantial proportion of your time in CCA-related activities (including training, competitions, outing etc.).

Of course, it is very important that you manage your time properly and commit sufficient time for school work, family life and rest.

Nevertheless, the general feedback is that you will enjoy your CCA, even though it may take up a large amount of your time.

Below are some guides on what you can expect in your CCA, as well as articles/testimonials of CCA life first-hand from students.

What you can expect to gain from your CCA

  1. Character Building

    Your CCA is bound to nuture you as a person, in one form or another, whether it is teaching you teamwork, determination, integrity or discipline.

  2. Lasting Friendships

    If you are passionate about what you do in your CCA, your CCA mates probably are too.

    Throughout your 4 years with your CCA buddies, you can be sure to forge lasting friendships them, especially since they share the same passion as you.

  3. New Skills

    In your CCA, you will be taught new skills and get to apply them in competitions, in projects and even your daily life.

  4. New Experiences & Exposure

    Through your CCA, you are sure to be exposed to new and exciting experiences, from competitions and outings to simply the weekly CCA experiences with your CCA buddies

Purpose of CCAs

CCAs are an integral part of a students' holistic, well-rounded education. It helps to develop the character, but also provides the students with opportunities to develop traits such as resilience, teamwork and leadership.

It helps to toughen their mental strength and not succumb to difficulties easily.

By working together in a team, CCAs can help develop team spirit and let students know how to co-operate with others. When students take on important roles in the CCAs, they will also be able to hone their leadership skills and learn to lead others.

No matter what CCA you get into, you should try to love and live with it, so that you can learn things that you are normally not able to learn in the curriculum.
