Scouts

Back to Uniform Groups

Scouting, is a worldwide youth movement with the aim of supporting young people in their physical, mental and spiritual development, so that they may play constructive roles in society. The movement employs the Scout method, a program of informal education with an emphasis on practical outdoor activities. Hwa Chong scout troop was set up in 1919. Up till now in 2012, the troop comprises of a Scout unit, a Venture scout unit, a Rover Scout unit and a scout alumni. Two of the most unique features in the HCI scout troop are the Bugle Band and the Lion Dance troupe, of which the former is the last of its kind in Singapore while the latter is also a very rare feature of scout troops in Singapore. Hwa Chong's Venture Scouts (HCVS) have done the school proud by sweeping 5 out of the 10 prestigious President's Scout Awards (PSA) given out nationwide in 2011. The President's Scout Award is presented to our nation's most outstanding Venture Scouts. It is given in recognition of their excellent leadership and personal performance, their dedication to the scout movement, as well as their service to the community. HCVS has produced 107 President's Scouts to date, a record amongst all schools in Singapore.

What we do

What we do is very different from what the other uniformed groups do. Instead of the basic foot drill, we do many interesting activities and this include cooking, hiking, first aid, knots, pioneering (building structures) and map reading. We also have 2 sub CCAs held on Fridays which are lion dance and bugle band respectively.
